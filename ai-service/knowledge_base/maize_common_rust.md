# Maize — Common Rust (Puccinia sorghi)

## Symptoms
Small, circular to elongate, cinnamon-brown pustules form on both leaf surfaces,
occasionally on stalks and husks. Pustules rupture the leaf epidermis, releasing
rust-colored spores. Severe infections cause leaves to yellow and die prematurely.

## Conditions favoring disease
Cool temperatures (16–23°C) combined with high humidity or extended leaf wetness
(dew, light rain). Common in high-altitude or highland maize-growing areas.

## Fertilizer recommendation
- Avoid excess nitrogen late in the season; it promotes dense, humid canopies
  that favor rust development.
- Balanced NPK fertilization (e.g. 17-17-17) applied at recommended agronomic
  rates supports plant vigor without encouraging excessive leafy growth.
- Adequate potassium improves stalk strength and general disease tolerance.

## Pesticide / fungicide recommendation
- Triazole-based fungicides (e.g. propiconazole, tebuconazole) are effective
  when applied at first sign of pustules.
- Strobilurin fungicides (e.g. azoxystrobin) can be used preventatively in
  high-risk seasons.
- Follow label rates and pre-harvest intervals strictly.

## Cultural practices
- Plant rust-resistant maize varieties where available.
- Avoid dense planting to improve airflow and reduce leaf wetness duration.
- Rotate with non-host crops to reduce inoculum buildup.

## Severity notes
Common rust rarely causes major yield loss on its own unless infection occurs
early in crop development (before tasseling) or coincides with another stress
such as drought.
