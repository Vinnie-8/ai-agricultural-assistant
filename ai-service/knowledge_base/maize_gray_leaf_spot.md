# Maize — Gray Leaf Spot (Cercospora zeae-maydis)

## Symptoms
Rectangular, tan-to-gray lesions bounded by leaf veins, running parallel to
the leaf blade. Lesions expand and merge in severe cases, causing large
sections of the leaf to die (blighting).

## Conditions favoring disease
Warm, humid weather with extended periods of leaf wetness. Common in
continuous maize cropping systems and under minimum/no-till where infected
residue remains on the soil surface.

## Fertilizer recommendation
- Ensure balanced nitrogen application — both nitrogen deficiency and excess
  can increase susceptibility; follow soil-test-based recommendations rather
  than over-applying.
- Adequate phosphorus and potassium support overall plant health and
  resistance to leaf diseases.

## Pesticide / fungicide recommendation
- Strobilurin or triazole fungicides applied at early disease onset (VT–R1
  growth stages) are most effective.
- Premix fungicides combining both modes of action offer stronger control
  in high-pressure seasons.

## Cultural practices
- Rotate with non-host crops (e.g. soybean) for at least one season.
- Till under infected residue to reduce spore survival, where feasible.
- Choose resistant or tolerant hybrids in fields with a history of the
  disease.

## Severity notes
Gray leaf spot can cause significant yield loss (10–50%) under favorable
conditions, especially in susceptible hybrids grown continuously in the
same field.
