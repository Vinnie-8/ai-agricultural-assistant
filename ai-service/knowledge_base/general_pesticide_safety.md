# General Pesticide & Fungicide Safety Guidance

## Before applying any pesticide or fungicide
- Always read and follow the product label — application rate, timing, and
  pre-harvest interval (PHI) vary by product and crop.
- Identify the disease or pest correctly before choosing a product; using
  the wrong product wastes money and may not control the problem.
- Rotate between different chemical classes/modes of action across a
  season to reduce the risk of resistance developing in the pathogen or
  pest population.

## Application timing
- Preventative applications (before disease appears) are generally more
  effective than curative ones, especially for fast-spreading diseases
  like late blight.
- Apply during calm weather, avoiding application immediately before rain
  (which can wash off the product) or during high heat/wind.

## Safety
- Always wear appropriate personal protective equipment (gloves, mask,
  long sleeves) when mixing and applying chemical products.
- Respect the pre-harvest interval (PHI) — the minimum time that must pass
  between the last application and harvest, to ensure produce is safe to
  eat.
- Store pesticides/fungicides away from children, animals, and food
  storage areas, in their original labeled containers.
- Wash hands and equipment thoroughly after application.

## Integrated approach
- Chemical treatment works best combined with cultural practices — crop
  rotation, resistant varieties, proper spacing, and sanitation (removing
  infected plant material) all reduce disease pressure and reduce how much
  chemical treatment is needed.
