# General Fertilizer Guidance

## NPK basics
- **Nitrogen (N)** — promotes leafy green growth. Excess nitrogen can make
  plants more susceptible to fungal disease by producing dense, humid
  canopies and softer tissue.
- **Phosphorus (P)** — supports root development and flowering/fruiting.
- **Potassium (K)** — improves overall plant vigor, disease resistance, and
  stress tolerance (drought, cold).

## General principles
- Base fertilizer application on soil test results where possible, rather
  than blanket application.
- Split nitrogen applications across the growing season rather than
  applying all at once, to avoid excess vegetative growth and disease
  risk.
- Over-fertilization is a common contributor to increased disease severity
  — more fertilizer is not always better.
- Organic matter (compost, manure) improves soil structure and nutrient
  availability over time, complementing synthetic fertilizers.

## When a plant is already diseased
- Avoid heavy nitrogen application to actively diseased plants — it can
  worsen fungal disease severity by promoting soft, susceptible tissue.
- Focus on potassium to support the plant's natural defenses while treating
  the disease directly with appropriate fungicide/pesticide.
