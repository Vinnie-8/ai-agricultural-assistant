# Maize — Northern Corn Leaf Blight (Exserohilum turcicum)

## Symptoms
Long, elliptical, gray-green to tan lesions (2.5–15 cm) resembling cigar
shapes, running parallel to leaf margins. Lesions may coalesce, giving
leaves a scorched, "blighted" appearance.

## Conditions favoring disease
Moderate temperatures (18–27°C) with heavy dew or extended humid periods.
Common during cool, wet growing seasons.

## Fertilizer recommendation
- Maintain balanced nitrogen — plants under nitrogen stress show increased
  lesion expansion and severity.
- Adequate potassium supports plant defense responses and stalk integrity,
  reducing lodging risk in heavily infected plants.

## Pesticide / fungicide recommendation
- Fungicides containing azoxystrobin, propiconazole, or pyraclostrobin are
  effective when applied at early lesion development.
- Time applications around tasseling (VT) for maximum protection of the
  ear leaf and leaves above it, which contribute most to yield.

## Cultural practices
- Use resistant hybrids (Ht genes) where blight pressure is historically
  high.
- Rotate away from maize for at least one year in heavily affected fields.
- Manage crop residue through tillage to reduce overwintering inoculum.

## Severity notes
Yield loss can range from minor to over 30% depending on how early
infection occurs relative to ear formation, and how susceptible the hybrid
is.
